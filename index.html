About the Project

Murch — Utility-Scale Solar

The Murch project is a utility-scale photovoltaic development engineered to deliver reliable, clean energy to the grid. Developed by Greensol Renewables, this project reflects our commitment to sustainable infrastructure, community benefit, and technical excellence.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about the Murch project, its technology, and community impact.

At 102 MW DC capacity with a single-axis tracking system, the Murch project is expected to generate approximately 320 GWh of clean electricity annually — enough to power over 45,000 average U.S. homes. See full technical specs.

The project's 320 GWh annual production is equivalent to powering approximately 45,000 homes per year based on average U.S. residential energy consumption of about 10,500 kWh/year.

Yes. The facility will be fully enclosed with perimeter fencing, security cameras, motion detection, and 24/7 remote monitoring via SCADA. Access control protocols restrict entry to authorized personnel only. Emergency response procedures are coordinated with local first responders.

Construction is expected to mobilize in Q1 2026 with full installation through Q3 2026, and commissioning concluding in Q4 2026 at the Commercial Operation Date (COD). View the full project timeline.

The Murch project will offset approximately 215,000 tons of CO₂ per year and save an estimated 2 million gallons of water annually compared to equivalent thermal power generation. It produces zero air emissions during operation.

The project is expected to create approximately 350 jobs during peak construction. A smaller number of permanent operations and maintenance positions will continue after the facility reaches commercial operation. Greensol prioritizes local hiring where qualified workers are available.

We welcome community feedback. You can reach our Community Liaison through the contact form, by email at j.spalding@green-sol.us, or by phone at +1 (786) 698 0814. We aim to respond within 48 hours.

Construction activity will be limited to daylight hours in compliance with local ordinances. We will proactively notify surrounding neighbors of significant activities. Once operational, the solar farm is virtually silent and does not generate light at night.
